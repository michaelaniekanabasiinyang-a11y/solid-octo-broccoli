How To Use This Plan

1. Learn First

Spend 25-35 minutes revising the topic: notes, flashcards, worked examples, vocabulary, case studies, dates, formulas, quotations, or key teachings.

2. Do Past Questions

Complete questions under timed conditions. Start with short questions, then build toward full papers in Weeks 9-12.

3. Mark And Fix

Use the mark scheme in a different colour. Write one correction, one model phrase, and one target for the next attempt.

12-Week Revision Map

Adjust the order to match your school’s exam dates. Keep the pattern: revise, practise, mark, redo.

Week 1

Baseline And Organisation

Download one recent paper or question set for each subject from your exam board. Complete 20-30 minutes per subject without notes to find weak areas.

  • Maths: one non-calculator mixed topic set.
  • English: one language analysis question and one literature paragraph.
  • Science: biology cell biology or organisation questions.
  • Spanish: vocabulary audit for family, school, free time, town, and holidays.
  • RE, Geography, History: one short-answer set each.
Week 2

Core Knowledge

Build memory foundations. Make flashcards for formulas, science required practicals, Spanish tenses, religious teachings, geography case-study facts, and history chronology.

  • Do 45 marks of Maths and 45 marks of Science questions.
  • Write one timed English Language response and one timed History 8 or 12 mark answer.
Week 3

Accuracy Week

Focus on command words: explain, analyse, evaluate, compare, describe, calculate, justify, and assess.

  • Maths: fractions, percentages, ratio, algebra basics, graph interpretation.
  • Science: chemistry atomic structure, bonding, quantitative chemistry basics.
  • Spanish: translation into English and short writing in three tenses.
Week 4

Paragraph Structure

Improve long answers. Use PEEL or PETAL paragraphs for English, RE, Geography, and History. Always add precise evidence.

  • English Literature: one extract response and one whole-text essay plan.
  • RE: one 12-mark evaluation answer with two sides and a justified conclusion.
  • Geography: one 6-mark and one 9-mark answer using a named case study.
Week 5

Timing Week

Use strict time limits. If you run out of time, finish in a different colour, then decide what you should have cut or planned faster.

  • Complete half a Maths paper, one English Language Section A, and one Spanish reading section.
  • Complete one Science topic question set for each of Biology, Chemistry, and Physics.
Week 6

Midpoint Review

Re-test Week 1 weak areas. Create a red-amber-green list and give green topics less time, amber topics regular practice, red topics teacher or tutor support.

  • Sit one full Maths paper or two half papers.
  • Write one full English essay and one full humanities long answer.
Week 7

High-Mark Questions

Practise the questions that decide grades: extended writing, multi-step maths, science required practicals, and evaluation questions.

  • Science: required practical methods, variables, graphs, and conclusions.
  • History: one source/interpretation question and one essay-style question.
  • Spanish: 90-word or 150-word writing response, depending on tier.
Week 8

Mixed Retrieval

Mix topics deliberately so your brain practises choosing methods. Do not revise only the topic you just practised.

  • Maths: calculator mixed paper questions.
  • Geography: physical and human geography mixed questions.
  • RE: beliefs, practices, themes, and ethical arguments mixed together.
Week 9

Full Paper Cycle 1

Complete full papers in exam conditions where possible. Mark within 24 hours and record the exact reason for each dropped mark.

  • Full Maths paper, full Science paper, and one full English Language section.
  • One full paper or section for Spanish, RE, Geography, and History.
Week 10

Full Paper Cycle 2

Repeat with a different paper. Your main goal is not just a higher score; it is fewer repeat mistakes.

  • Redo every question you lost marks on before trying new material.
  • Memorise 10 Spanish phrases, 10 English quotations, and 10 history/geography facts.
Week 11

Exam Readiness

Practise under realistic conditions: quiet desk, timer visible, no phone, no notes, mark scheme afterwards.

  • Two full papers or timed sections for your weakest subjects.
  • One light confidence set for your strongest subjects.
Week 12

Final Polish

Do not cram everything. Focus on memory sheets, corrected questions, model answers, formulas, case studies, quotations, and exam technique.

  • Complete short timed sets only. Stop heavy work the evening before each exam.
  • Read correction logs and practise calm planning before writing.

Subject Past-Question Plan

English Language And Literature

AnalysisEvaluationCreative writingEssays

Weekly practice: one reading question, one writing task, one literature paragraph, and one quotation recall test.

Past-question focus: language methods, structure, comparison, writer’s viewpoint, unseen extract response, poetry comparison, character/theme essays.

Marking rule: highlight evidence, terminology, explanation, context, and judgement separately.

Maths

FluencyProblem solvingCalculatorNon-calculator

Weekly practice: 45-60 marks of mixed questions, then redo every incorrect question two days later.

Past-question focus: number, algebra, ratio, geometry, probability, statistics, graphs, transformations, equations, and multi-step problem solving.

Marking rule: write the first line you should have written, not just the final answer.

Science

BiologyChemistryPhysicsRequired practicals

Weekly practice: one biology set, one chemistry set, one physics set, plus one required practical question.

Past-question focus: command words, calculations, graph skills, practical methods, variables, conclusions, and extended 6-mark explanations.

Marking rule: copy the exact scientific term you missed into a glossary.

Spanish

ReadingListeningWritingSpeaking

Weekly practice: two reading/listening sets, one translation, one writing answer, and one speaking photo-card or role-play.

Past-question focus: tenses, opinions, reasons, negatives, time phrases, question words, topic vocabulary, and complex structures.

Marking rule: keep a list of reusable phrases and rewrite one answer with better verbs and connectives.

Religious Education

BeliefsPracticesThemesEvaluation

Weekly practice: two short questions, one explain question, and one 12-mark evaluation response.

Past-question focus: key teachings, scripture or authority references, contrasting views, ethical reasoning, and justified conclusions.

Marking rule: every long answer needs two developed sides and a clear final judgement.

Geography

PhysicalHumanFieldworkCase studies

Weekly practice: map/graph skill question, one case-study question, one fieldwork question, and one 6 or 9 marker.

Past-question focus: data interpretation, named examples, processes, impacts, management strategies, and evaluate questions.

Marking rule: add place-specific detail: numbers, locations, named schemes, and real impacts.

History

ChronologyCausationSourcesInterpretations

Weekly practice: one chronology recall, one source or interpretation question, and one essay-style answer.

Past-question focus: causes, consequences, change, continuity, significance, source utility, interpretations, and precise factual knowledge.

Marking rule: each paragraph needs a clear claim, accurate evidence, and a link back to the question wording.

Correction Log Template

Question: paper, year, question number.

Lost marks because: knowledge gap, timing, misread command word, weak evidence, calculation error, unclear paragraph, missing units, or spelling/grammar.

Fixed answer: write the improved line, method, paragraph, formula, fact, quote, or phrase.

Retest date: two days later, then one week later.

Exam-Day Habits

Read the command word twice. Underline the exact topic. Plan long answers briefly. Show working in Maths and Science. Use named examples in Geography. Use accurate evidence in History. In Spanish, include opinions, reasons, time phrases, and at least two tenses.
